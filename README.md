# Void Cartography

**An epistemological method for detecting ontological layer mismatch in stuck problems.**

When a scientific or philosophical problem has been worked on for decades without resolution, the difficulty may not be complexity or lack of data. It may be that the question is being asked at one ontological level while the answer lives at a different level entirely. Void Cartography provides a structured method for detecting this mismatch and generating testable hypotheses about where the answer actually lives.

## The Paper Sequence

This repository contains a four-paper series that develops the method from philosophical foundations through formalization to live demonstration.

### Paper 18 -- The Whirlpool Lineage: Pattern Persistence Across Substrates
The philosophical foundation. Traces the idea that identity lives in pattern rather than material through 2,500 years of thought -- from Heraclitus through Buddhist anicca, Whitehead's process philosophy, Prigogine's dissipative structures, Maturana and Varela's autopoiesis, to Hofstadter's strange loops. Shows what each thinker advanced and what each left unfinished. Establishes the intellectual lineage that the Realima Taxonomy (Whitman, 2024) completes.

### Paper 19 -- Void Cartography: Finding Something in the Nothing (V4)
The core method paper. Introduces Exclusion Inversion: build a structured dataset of what the answer is not (excluria), read the shape of the empty space (formavacua), then execute the category-flip (versio) to generate a hypothesis about which ontological layer the answer occupies. Defines a seven-term Latin vocabulary, four versio outputs (Capacium, Processium, Emergentium, Relatio), and two quantitative tools -- the Categorical Exhaustion Ratio (CER) and the Property-Violation Test. Validated against five case studies including a confirmed false positive (prion diseases). Reviewed independently by GPT-4o, DeepSeek-R1, and Gemini 2.5 Pro.

### Paper 20 -- The Handle: A Naming Protocol for the Unknown (V3)
The naming layer. When you discover something that existing vocabulary cannot name without distorting, how do you name it? Introduces the Interface Principle, five failure modes for scientific terminology (Premature Identity, Category Smuggling, Metaphor Fossilization, Audience Lock-In, Resolution Mismatch), a six-step naming protocol, and a deprecation lifecycle. Applied to the Realima Taxonomy and CRISPR as case studies.

### Paper 21 -- Void Cartography as an Instrument
The live demonstration. Runs Void Cartography in real time on three unsolved problems simultaneously: the hard problem of consciousness, AI alignment, and the dark proteome. Shows the method discriminating across domains -- three different problems produce three different versio outputs (consciousness to Relatio/Processium split, alignment to Emergentium, dark proteome to Capacium). Formalizes four operational modes.

## Key Concepts

Ontological layer mismatch -- A problem is stuck because research is conducted at one level of reality (objects, forces, mechanisms) while the answer lives at a different level (enabling conditions, processes, emergent properties, relational structures).

Exclusion Inversion -- Instead of searching for what the answer is, systematically catalog what it is not. The shape of the exclusions reveals the ontological layer of the answer.

Versio taxonomy -- Four possible outputs: Capacium (enabling condition), Processium (systemic flow), Emergentium (consequent property), Relatio (connectivity). Independently convergent with Bunge's CESM model (1979).

Categorical Exhaustion Ratio (CER) -- Measures what fraction of frameworks (not papers) within an excluded category have been tried and failed. CER > 0.75 = coherent signal.

Property-Violation Test -- Discriminates genuine ontological layer mismatch from incomplete taxonomy within the correct layer.
