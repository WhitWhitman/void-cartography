## 1. The Language Problem

Before any method can work, the words have to exist.

When K.E. Whitman developed the Realima Taxonomy (2024), he ran into a wall that had nothing to do with the science. The concepts he needed to express did not have words. Existing vocabulary carried existing assumptions. Call an AI system a "tool" and you've already decided what it is. Call a pattern-maintaining process a "program" and you've collapsed the ontology before the investigation starts. So he went to Latin, built new terms from roots, and said: this is what we're calling these things until better terms arrive.

This paper faces the same problem, and addresses it using a formal naming protocol described in the companion paper (Paper 20, The Handle). The first two drafts were reviewed by three independent AI systems (GPT-4o, DeepSeek-R1, Gemini 2.5 Pro). All three attacked the same point: the method's outputs kept getting described as "infrastructure," "rooms," and "context." Those are nouns. They are objects. The method claims to escape object-bias and then named its results with object-words. Gemini put it cleanest: "You are successfully escaping the search for a participant, but you might just be building a larger box."

The vocabulary was the flaw. V3 fixed it. V4 grounds it.

## 2. Core Vocabulary

Seven terms built from Latin roots following the Handle protocol (Paper 20). Each root maps to a confirmed boundary, not a theory. Provisional. Replaceable. Necessary.

**Excluria** (eks-KLOOR-ee-ah). From *excludere* (to shut out) + *-ia* (collected body of). The structured dataset of confirmed negatives for a given problem. Not void, not absence. An excluria is full. Every confirmed NOT is a data point. The collection has topology, density, and coherence. Building an excluria is the first operation.

**Formavacua** (FOR-mah-VAH-koo-ah). From *forma* (shape) + *vacua* (empty). The coherent outline that emerges when an excluria is read as a set. The shape of the empty. When the negatives cluster around a single ontological category, the formavacua becomes visible. A chalk outline around a missing body.

**Versio** (VER-see-oh). From *vertere* (to turn). The category-flip. The cognitive operation in which the formavacua resolves into a hypothesis about a different ontological layer. Not deduction, not induction, not standard abduction. A specific move: reading what category the exclusions consistently describe, and inferring that the answer lies in a different category.

**Capacium** (kah-PAH-see-um). From *capax* (able to hold) + *-ium* (that which). One of four versio outputs. The static enabling condition. The ability-to-hold that precedes whatever is held. Identified when the formavacua is shaped by excluded *actors* or *participants* â things that interact and cause. When all the doers are excluded, what remains is the condition that lets doing happen. Literature home: constraint-based systems theory (Deacon, Juarrero), boundary conditions, enabling conditions, conditions of possibility.

**Processium** (proh-CESS-ee-um). From *processus* (advance, course) + *-ium* (that which). Second versio output. The dynamic enabling condition. The systemic flow that carries things without pushing them. Identified when the formavacua is shaped by excluded *forces* or *discrete mechanisms* â things that push, pull, or directly cause. When all the pushers are excluded, what remains is a process that enables movement without applying force. Literature home: process philosophy (Whitehead, Rescher, Seibt), dynamical systems, flow-based explanation.

**Emergentium** (eh-mer-GEN-tee-um). From *emergens* (arising out of) + *-ium* (that which). Third versio output. The consequent property. What arises from all parts operating simultaneously without any part controlling it. Identified when the formavacua is shaped by excluded *determinants* or *controllers* â things that set, regulate, or decide a value. When all the controllers are excluded, what remains is a property that emerges from the whole. Literature home: emergence theory (Broad, Alexander, Kim, Bedau, O'Connor), system-level resultant properties.

**Relatio** (reh-LAH-tee-oh). From *relatio* (a bringing back, connection). Fourth versio output. The connective condition. What exists between nodes rather than within them. Identified when the formavacua is shaped by excluded *components* or *individual nodes.* When all the individual parts are excluded as insufficient, what remains is the connectivity itself. Literature home: ontic structural realism, relational ontology, network metaphysics. Validated in this version (see Section 8.5).

No single existing taxonomy cleanly yields these four outputs as a set. The closest independent precedent is Bunge's CESM model of systems (Bunge, 1979, 2003), which holds that any system requires four descriptors: Composition, Environment, Structure, and Mechanism. The mapping is striking: Capacium corresponds to Environment (the enabling constraints a system is connected to), Processium to Mechanism (characteristic processes that make the system what it is), Emergentium to emergent properties (system-level properties that parts do not possess), and Relatio to Structure (relations among components and between components and environment). CESM was derived from asking "what must any system have?" Void Cartography's taxonomy was derived from asking "what remains when the objects are excluded?" Two different starting points, convergent fourfold. This convergence suggests the taxonomy is not arbitrary â it picks out orthogonal explanatory axes that are independently motivated in systems ontology.

The versio taxonomy is nevertheless proposed as a provisional operational grid rather than a recovered historical schema. These four recur because they are the most common ways scientific explanation shifts away from discrete objects: toward what enables, what unfolds, what arises, and what connects. Formal ontology frameworks (BFO, DOLCE) provide additional anchoring for three of the four outputs â dispositions/capabilities for Capacium, occurrent processes for Processium, qualities/properties for Emergentium â though neither yields a clean one-to-one fourfold matching all four.

## 3. The Method

Void Cartography is a late-stage epistemological method for detecting ontological layer mismatch in stuck problems. "Layer mismatch" means: the question is being asked at one ontological level (objects, forces, mechanisms) while the answer lives at a different level (enabling conditions, processes, emergent properties, relational structures). The method generates specific, testable hypotheses about which layer the answer occupies. Domain specialists do the actual investigation within the redirected layer.

Its core operation, Exclusion Inversion, has three steps.

**Step 1: Build the Excluria.** Catalog every confirmed negative for the problem. Empirically confirmed, observationally verified, experimentally ruled out. Each entry must meet the standard: a competent researcher in the field would agree this has been excluded. A thin excluria (few confirmed negatives, young problem) means the method cannot operate. A dense excluria (decades of accumulated exclusions across multiple independent research programmes) is the signal that the method may be applicable.

**Step 2: Read the Formavacua.** Examine the excluria as a set. The question is not "what's left?" (that's Holmes). The question is: do the excluded items share an ontological category? The detection mechanism is **failure symmetry**: the observation that the same *type* of answer keeps failing across independent research programmes. Not the same answer. The same *type.*

Failure symmetry does not appear, to our knowledge, as a named concept in the existing philosophy-of-science literature. A 177-source deep research survey (GPT-4o, April 2026) confirmed no widely used, formalized term whose central definition is "the same answer-type fails across independent research programmes." The concept is, however, structurally adjacent to four traditions â three philosophical, one engineering.

In philosophy of science: Laudan's research traditions (1977) showed that multiple theories can share deep background commitments about what kind of answer counts; failure symmetry reads the pattern when those shared commitments produce coordinated failure. Stanford's unconceived alternatives (2006) explained why scientists repeatedly fail to imagine viable options outside their shared assumptions; failure symmetry is the diagnostic signature of exactly that blind spot. Wimsatt's robustness reasoning showed that independent methods converging on the same result raise confidence; failure symmetry is the negative mirror â independent methods failing in the same ontological way raise suspicion that the shared category assumption, not any individual candidate, is the obstacle. This could be termed *negative robustness* â the systematic convergence of independent failure onto a single ontological type â though that phrase is our extension, not an established term.

In reliability engineering: **common-mode failure** is defined as multiple redundant subsystems failing in the same way for the same reason, typically due to a shared design vulnerability or latent cause. The structural match to failure symmetry is tight. Independent research programmes are intended to be redundant â different theoretical approaches to the same problem. But if they share a hidden vulnerability (the same ontological category assumption), the redundancy fails. The failures become correlated through the shared cause, not through any connection between the programmes themselves. Failure symmetry is common-mode failure translated from engineering systems to epistemic systems: independent-looking inquiry defeated by hidden common constraints at the category level.

**Formal trigger criterion:** When three or more independent research programmes (different theoretical frameworks, different experimental approaches) addressing the same problem have produced confirmed negative results, and those negatives share an ontological category, the formavacua is coherent enough to attempt a versio.

**Step 3: Execute the Versio.** If the formavacua is coherent, identify which ontological category the exclusions describe, and infer which category the answer probably occupies. This is not a free-form flip. The versio taxonomy constrains the output:

| Formavacua Shape | Excluded Category | Versio Output | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Actors, participants, interacting entities | Objects that do things | **Capacium** | Static enabling condition |
| Forces, discrete mechanisms, direct causes | Things that push or determine | **Processium** | Dynamic systemic flow |
| Determinants, controllers, regulators | Things that set values | **Emergentium** | Consequent property of the whole |
| Individual components, nodes | Parts taken separately | **Relatio** | Connectivity between parts |

The taxonomy makes the versio discriminating rather than defaulting. V1 of this paper produced "capacium" for every problem because capacium was the only output category. V4 routes each problem through the taxonomy based on the specific shape of its formavacua. Different shapes, different outputs.

**What the method predicts:** For dark matter, it predicts that detection strategies optimized for particle interactions (direct detection, collider production) will continue to underperform relative to structural-effect strategies (gravitational lensing, large-scale structure surveys). For cell size, it predicts that no single "size gene" or "size checkpoint" exists; size will be reproduced in silico only when all cellular processes are modeled simultaneously. For the theory of everything, it predicts that programmes treating the TOE as a specific equation to be discovered will continue to stall, while programmes investigating the conditions under which equations become valid will make more progress. These predictions are falsifiable by domain-specific experiments.

## 4. What Void Cartography Is Not

**It is not falsificationism (Popper, 1934).** Falsificationism eliminates candidate answers within an ontological category. Void Cartography operates *on* ontological categories, detecting when the entire category is wrong.

**It is not Lakatosian programme analysis (Lakatos, 1978).** Lakatos used failure trajectories to evaluate which programme is healthier. Void Cartography uses failure patterns to identify when all programmes share the same categorical assumption, and that assumption may be the obstacle.

**It is not a Kuhnian paradigm shift (Kuhn, 1962).** Kuhn described category changes after they happened (phlogiston to oxygen, Ptolemaic to Copernican, caloric to kinetic theory). Void Cartography attempts to detect when a category change is needed before the shift occurs, by reading the excluria that Kuhn's anomalies leave behind.

**It is not an inverse problem â but it is the inverse problem's diagnostic cousin.** Inverse problems (seismic tomography, CT imaging, electrical resistance tomography) infer hidden structure from observable constraints. Both methods extract positive knowledge from constraint patterns. The difference: inverse problems reconstruct hidden structure of the same ontological type as the observations. Void Cartography detects when the hidden structure is a *different ontological type.* It is the inverse problem that fires when standard inverse methods cannot converge because the latent structure does not have the form they assume.

A concrete case illustrates the boundary. In the 1980s, geophysicists used seismic tomography to map the Earth's mantle by measuring earthquake wave velocities. The mathematics assumed the mantle's velocity was an isotropic scalar â an intrinsic, fixed property of the rock. The inverse problem refused to converge. Vertical waves through a region reported one velocity; horizontal waves through the same rock reported another. If velocity were a fixed object-property, the data contradicted itself. The resolution: researchers changed the parameter from a scalar (property of an object) to a tensor (a relational condition dependent on the direction of interaction). The rock's crystals align under tectonic stress; wave speed depends on how the wave's path relates to crystal alignment. Once the inverse problem was rewritten to infer a tensor rather than a scalar, the mathematics converged instantly, revealing the flowing deformation of the Earth's interior. The answer was not a property of the rock itself but a condition of the environment â a shift from object to relation. This is the mathematical mirror of a versio: the standard inverse method assumed an Actor-level parameter and failed; the fix required shifting to a Relatio/Capacium-level parameter.

**It is not apophatic theology, neti neti, or via negativa.** These share the negation move but differ in destination. Apophatic theology terminates in transcendence. Neti neti terminates in liberation. Via negativa terminates in practical wisdom. Void Cartography terminates in a specific, testable, category-level hypothesis that can be wrong.

## 5. Philosophical Anchors

The claim that inquiry can fail by targeting the wrong ontological layer is not introduced ex nihilo. It stands near several established traditions. What Void Cartography adds is a way of detecting such mismatch from the patterned structure of accumulated failures.

### 5.1 Ontological Layers

**Nicolai Hartmann** (Der Aufbau der realen Welt, 1940) built an explicit ontology of strata: inorganic, organic, psychic, spiritual. Higher layers depend on lower ones but are not reducible to them. Hartmann provides the philosophical framework for a stratified reality where different levels have different categories and laws. He does not formalize a method for detecting when research is stuck at the wrong stratum. Void Cartography supplies the detector.

**Carl Craver** (Explaining the Brain, 2007; Machamer, Darden, and Craver, 2000) makes levels operational inside explanation. Different mechanisms sit at different levels. Good explanations respect level relations; bad ones confuse them. Craver provides a disciplined literature on interlevel relations and what goes wrong when levels are mixed. However, Craver works primarily within a mechanistic explanatory framework. Void Cartography explicitly needs room for non-mechanistic outputs (capacium, relatio) that fall outside mechanism.

**David Marr** (Vision, 1982) provides the classic triad: computational level, algorithmic/representational level, implementational level. This is perhaps the most famous formal account of why a question can be valid at one level and misguided at another. For readers outside philosophy, Marr may be the most intuitively graspable anchor for "wrong level, wrong answer."

**The mereological fallacy** (Bennett and Hacker, 2003) is one of the cleanest "wrong level" concepts available: attributing to parts what can only intelligibly be attributed to wholes. Asking what the neuron "believes." Asking what the gene "decides." Asking what a part does that only makes sense at the system level. This is narrower than Void Cartography's full scope but highly resonant with the emergentium output.

**Oppenheim and Putnam** ("Unity of Science as a Working Hypothesis," 1958) proposed a system of reductive levels ordered from elementary particles through social groups, and explicitly flagged as methodologically suspect any attempt to reduce a whole branch while *skipping* the immediately lower level. Even outside strict reductionism, this is a clean citation showing that serious philosophy of science already treats "wrong-level" moves as methodological errors.

**Wimsatt's causal thickets** (1994, 2007) describe what happens when level-talk breaks down in complex systems: boundary ambiguities proliferate, disputes multiply over how the system should be carved, and the level at which the phenomenon "lives" becomes genuinely contested. This is the vocabulary for the stuck state Void Cartography diagnoses â the condition where a field has been asking the wrong kind of question long enough that the confusion has become structural.

**Grounding** (non-causal constitutive determination) provides an additional anchor. Some "why" questions are causal (what pushed it?) and some are constitutive (what makes it what it is?). Conflating them yields stuckness. This formalizes a distinction implicit in the versio taxonomy: Capacium and Relatio tend to answer constitutive "why" questions (what enables? what connects?), while Processium answers causal "why" questions at a different scale, and Emergentium answers neither â it responds to "what arises?" not "why?" The layer mismatch is sometimes not just "wrong level of stuff" but "wrong kind of why."

### 5.2 Failure Symmetry

**Laudan's research traditions** (Progress and Its Problems, 1977) work above the level of individual theories. His traditions carry shared assumptions, methods, and standards across multiple theories. Failure symmetry can be framed as a diagnostic pattern showing that multiple programmes share the same background commitment about what kind of answer counts â and that commitment is the obstacle.

**Stanford's unconceived alternatives** (Exceeding Our Grasp, 2006) explains why scientists repeatedly fail to imagine viable alternatives: shared background assumptions make certain solution-types invisible. The prion case is a direct instance. Stanford gives the literature for the general blind spot; failure symmetry gives the diagnostic signature.

**Wimsatt's robustness** (1980s onward) holds that when independent methods converge on the same result, confidence rises. Failure symmetry is the negative mirror: when independent methods fail in the same ontological way, suspicion shifts from candidate answers to the shared category assumption. This could be termed *negative robustness* â the systematic convergence of independent failure onto a single ontological type â though that phrase is our extension, not an established term.

### 5.3 Versio Taxonomy

No single existing taxonomy cleanly maps onto all four versio outputs. The closest independent precedent is Bunge's CESM model (Section 2), which yields a near-isomorphism: Environment â Capacium, Mechanism â Processium, emergent system-properties â Emergentium, Structure â Relatio. But CESM describes what any system *has*; the versio taxonomy describes what *remains* when objects are excluded. Same fourfold, different derivation.

Beyond CESM, partial mappings anchor each output in established traditions:

**Capacium** finds its nearest home in constraint-based systems theory (Deacon, Juarrero), boundary conditions, enabling conditions, conditions of possibility, and the BFO concept of dispositions/capabilities (realizable entities conditionally manifested).

**Processium** is grounded in process philosophy (Whitehead, Rescher, Seibt), dynamical systems theory, flow-based explanation, and the BFO/DOLCE category of occurrent processes (realizations of dispositional capacities).

**Emergentium** is anchored in emergence theory (Broad, Alexander, Kim, Bedau, O'Connor), system-level resultant properties, and the DOLCE quality category (qualities borne by higher-level entities).

**Relatio** maps to ontic structural realism, relational ontology, network/structural metaphysics, and the relations literature in contemporary analytic metaphysics (relations as irreducible to monadic properties).

These four recur because they pick out orthogonal explanatory axes already recognized in the literature: what enables, what unfolds, what arises, and what connects. Remove any one and you lose coverage of a major axis. The versio taxonomy is proposed as a provisional operational grid empirically motivated by the validation cases and independently supported by systems ontology (CESM), not a recovered historical schema.

## 6. The Five-Problem Test (Reclassified)

The method was invented and tested simultaneously, in a live session, April 3, 2026. K.E. Whitman proposed the logic, then fed five unsolved problems in real time.

V4 reclassifies each problem through the versio taxonomy. Not all five are capacium. The taxonomy discriminates.

### 6.1 Dark Matter

**Excluria:** Not baryonic. Not electromagnetic. Not hot. Not disk-concentrated. Not small-scale clumpy. Not fast. Not strongly self-interacting. Not rapidly decaying. Not zero-mass.

**Formavacua shape:** Every exclusion describes a *participant* â something that interacts via Standard Model forces.

**Versio output: Capacium.** The answer is probably not a participant. It is probably an enabling condition for galactic structure that does not participate in Standard Model interactions.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Detection strategies optimized for participant-type interactions (direct detection, collider production) will continue to underperform relative to structural-effect strategies (gravitational lensing, large-scale structure surveys, cosmological probes). If a standard WIMP is detected by a direct-detection experiment, the method produced a false positive on this problem.

### 6.2 Theory of Everything

**Excluria:** Not a single equation. Not quantum gravity alone. Not background-dependent. Not perturbative. Not classical. Not fully discrete. Not fully continuous. Not observer-free.

**Formavacua shape:** Every exclusion describes a *formal object* â a specific type of mathematical structure.

**Versio output: Capacium.** The answer is probably not a formal object. It is probably the enabling condition under which formal objects become possible.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Research programmes that treat the TOE as an equation to be discovered will continue to stall. Programmes that investigate the conditions under which equations become valid (background-independence research, category-theoretic approaches, pre-geometric frameworks) will make more progress. If the TOE turns out to be a specific equation discovered by conventional mathematical search, the method produced a false positive.

### 6.3 Dimensionless Physical Constants

**Excluria:** Not derivable. Not random. Not unit-dependent. Not time-varying (significantly). Not reducible. Not mathematically necessary. Not physically explained.

**Formavacua shape:** Every exclusion describes a *parameter* â something that could be tuned or derived.

**Versio output: Capacium.** The constants are probably not parameters. They are probably the enabling condition that defines the possibility space where physics operates.

**Falsifiable prediction:** If a derivation is found that explains any dimensionless constant from within a parameter-tuning framework (e.g., landscape selection), the method's output is wrong for that constant.

### 6.4 The Dark Proteome

**Excluria:** Not enzymes (mostly). Not structural (mostly). Not signaling molecules (mostly). Not membrane-bound (mostly). Not highly expressed. Not individually essential. Not conserved like functional proteins.

**Formavacua shape:** Every exclusion describes a *doer* â a protein with a discrete function.

**Versio output: Capacium.** The unknown proteins are probably not doers. They are probably the enabling condition that makes protein function possible.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Research that asks "what does this protein DO?" will continue to fail for the dark proteome. Research that asks "what happens to the cellular environment when this class degrades?" will find the signal. If individual dark-proteome members are shown to have discrete, catalogable molecular functions, the method is wrong.

### 6.5 Cell Size Determination

**Excluria:** Not genome size (mostly). Not any single gene. Not nutrient supply alone. Not division timing alone. Not surface-area-to-volume ratio alone. Not mechanical constraint. Not any single checkpoint.

**Formavacua shape:** Every exclusion describes a *determinant* â a discrete controller that could set cell size.

**Versio output: Emergentium.** Cell size is probably not determined by any mechanism. It is probably a consequent property of all cellular processes operating simultaneously. No single controller exists because size emerges from the whole. The formavacua here is shaped by excluded *controllers*, not excluded *participants*, which routes it to emergentium rather than capacium.

**Falsifiable prediction:** Research looking for "the size gene" or "the size checkpoint" will continue to fail. Research modeling size as an emergent property of coupled intracellular dynamics will converge. If a single master regulator of cell size is identified, the method is wrong.

**Note on V1/V2 error:** Previous versions classified cell size as capacium. The versio taxonomy corrects this. The excluded category is controllers (determinants), not actors (participants). A capacium enables by providing conditions; an emergentium arises from the system without any enabling condition being "for" it. Different ontological layer.

## 7. Validation Against Solved Problems

### 7.1 Helicobacter pylori and Peptic Ulcers (Object-Shaped Answer)

Pre-discovery excluria: Not stress alone. Not diet alone. Not acid alone.

**Formavacua:** Mixed. Exclusions describe mechanisms and conditions, not consistently actors or forces. No coherent formavacua emerges.

**Versio:** Does not fire. Formavacua incoherent. The method produces no output.

**Assessment:** Correct non-application. The answer was an object (a bacterium). The method did not produce a false non-object. **Pass.**

### 7.2 The Higgs Boson (Object-Shaped Answer)

Pre-2012 excluria: Not at these energy levels. Not at those energy levels.

**Formavacua:** No ontological shape. Exclusions are parametric (where on the energy spectrum), not categorical (what type of thing).

**Versio:** Cannot operate. Excluria is not ontologically dense.

**Assessment:** Correct refusal. The Higgs was an object, the search was always object-level, and the method correctly identifies it has nothing to contribute. **Pass.**

### 7.3 Continental Drift (Non-Object Answer)

Pre-1960s excluria: Not tidal forces. Not centrifugal forces. Not thermal contraction. Not expanding Earth. Not any force applied to continents directly.

**Formavacua shape:** Every exclusion describes a *force* â a discrete mechanism that pushes.

**Versio output: Processium.** The answer is probably not a force. It is probably a systemic flow that carries things without pushing them.

**Actual answer:** Mantle convection currents carrying plates as passengers. A process. A flow. The continents are not pushed by a force; they ride on a dynamic system. This is structurally a processium.

**Assessment:** Genuine hit. The versio taxonomy correctly routes this to processium (not capacium, as V2 misclassified it). The formavacua was force-shaped, not actor-shaped. The distinction demonstrates the taxonomy's discriminative power. **Pass.**

**V2 correction:** V2 classified continental drift as capacium. Gemini's review flagged that mantle convection is a process, not a static enabling condition. The versio taxonomy corrects this: excluded forces â processium, not excluded actors â capacium. This is the first concrete evidence that the taxonomy adds discriminative value over a single-output model.

### 7.4 Prion Diseases (Object-Shaped Answer â Detailed Analysis)

Pre-Prusiner excluria (before 1982): Not a virus (no nucleic acid found â Alper's UV inactivation work, 1960s). Not a bacterium (too small, filterable). Not a fungus (no fungal morphology). Not a protozoan (no protozoan structures). Not a toxin (transmissible, so not a static agent). Not a viroid (no RNA detected). Not an immune complex (no immunoglobulin in purified preparations). Not any known pathogen class.

**Step-by-step application of formal criteria:**

*Ontological classification:* Each exclusion targets a specific type of infectious agent. Virus = Actor. Bacterium = Actor. Fungus = Actor. Protozoan = Actor. Toxin = Actor (a causal entity, even if chemical). Viroid = Actor. Immune complex = Actor. The exclusions are overwhelmingly Actor-shaped.

*Coherence score:* 8 of 8 exclusions classify as Actor. That is 100%. The threshold (Section 10) requires >75%. The formavacua is coherent.

*Density:* 8 confirmed negatives. The minimum is 8. Pass.

*Independence:* Alper's UV inactivation programme (UK, 1960s), Griffith's protein-only hypothesis (theoretical, 1967), Prusiner's early purification work (1970s), multiple independent labs attempting nucleic acid isolation. At least 3 independent programmes. Pass.

*Versio trigger:* All criteria satisfied. The versio fires. Output: Capacium.

**Actual answer:** A prion â a misfolded protein that IS an object, IS a pathogenic agent, just a radically different kind of agent than anything previously recognized. The answer was a novel Actor outside the existing taxonomy of Actors.

**Assessment:** False positive. The method correctly followed its own criteria and produced the wrong answer. This is not a misapplication of the method; it is a genuine structural limitation. The formal criteria were satisfied (coherent formavacua, sufficient density, independent programmes), the versio was correctly triggered, and it pointed to the wrong layer. **Fail.**

**What this teaches:** The method cannot distinguish between "the answer is in a different ontological layer" (versio is correct) and "the answer is in the same layer but outside the current classification" (versio is a false positive). When an entire taxonomy of objects is excluded, it could mean the answer is not an object or that the taxonomy needs a new entry. The method has no internal way to tell these two cases apart from the excluria alone.

**Prion discriminator (the Property-Violation Test):** A partial external discriminator exists. Examine the *nature* of the exclusions in the excluria:

- **Compositional exclusions** target specific instances or compositions of a category. Pre-1982 biologists excluded pathogens made of DNA and RNA. They did not exclude the fundamental properties of an Actor: spatial localization, physical transmission, discrete interaction. Because the fundamental *properties* of the category were never excluded, the formavacua was incomplete. The answer was still an Actor. This signals **elevated risk of incomplete taxonomy.**

- **Functional exclusions** target the defining properties of the category itself. In cell size, biology did not just rule out specific "size genes" (instances); research ruled out the existence of a single localized control mechanism entirely. Ruling out the *concept* of control violates the boundary of the Determinant category. This signals **genuine layer mismatch.**

**The rule:** If the excluria only eliminates known compositions or instances within a category (e.g., "no DNA-based pathogen," "no mass > X"), flag as high risk for incomplete taxonomy â the versio may be a false positive. If the excluria eliminates the functional definition of the category (e.g., "no localized controller exists," "no force transmission occurs"), the versio is more likely to represent genuine layer mismatch.

This discriminator is not built into the method's formal criteria. It is an external check that the analyst applies after the versio fires, before acting on the output. It does not eliminate the false-positive risk; it stratifies it.

### 7.5 Quantum Entanglement (Non-Object Answer â Relatio Validation)

Pre-resolution excluria: Not predetermined node states (Bell's Theorem and subsequent experimental tests ruled out local hidden variables â the particles do not carry the "answer" with them from the source). Not a traveling mechanism (the no-communication theorem and Aspect's experiments rule out faster-than-light signaling â there is no force, wave, or particle traveling between nodes to coordinate them). Not a temporal sequence (delayed-choice quantum eraser experiments rule out the idea that the state of one node is determined chronologically "before" the other).

**Formavacua shape:** The exclusions target two ontological categories simultaneously. Local hidden variables = Components/Nodes (properties intrinsic to the individual parts). FTL signals = Mechanisms (discrete causes traveling between points). The void outlines the individual nodes and the space between them. The excluria proves the answer is not inside the particle and not pushing through the space between particles.

**Versio output: Relatio.** The excluded categories are Components and Mechanisms. When individual parts are excluded as insufficient AND discrete connecting mechanisms are excluded, what remains is the connectivity itself. The fundamental reality of entangled particles is not the particles (nodes) or a signal (mechanism) â it is the mathematical and physical relationship itself. The connection is the primary ontological object. The particles are endpoints where the relatio interacts with measurement tools.

**Assessment:** Genuine hit. The formavacua routes cleanly to Relatio. This is the first validated case for the fourth versio output. The exclusion pattern is distinctive: unlike the other cases, entanglement's excluria excludes both component-properties and inter-component mechanisms simultaneously, which is precisely the pattern that the taxonomy predicts should route to Relatio. **Pass.**

## 9. Failure Symmetry: Formal Criteria

The detection engine needs operational teeth. Here is a working specification.

### 9.1 Category Taxonomy

**Definition:** Failure symmetry exists when three or more independent research programmes addressing the same problem have produced confirmed negative results, and those negatives can be classified into a single ontological category using the following taxonomy:

| Category Label | What It Contains | Example Exclusions |
|---|---|---|
| Actors / Participants | Things that interact, cause, do | "Not this particle," "not this protein," "not this gene" |
| Forces / Mechanisms | Things that push, determine, directly cause | "Not this force," "not gravity," "not thermal expansion" |
| Determinants / Controllers | Things that set, regulate, switch | "Not this gene," "not this checkpoint," "not nutrient supply" |
| Components / Nodes | Individual parts taken separately | "Not this neuron," "not this node," "not this element" |

### 9.2 Coherence Scoring: The Categorical Exhaustion Ratio

Counting individual negative results â or even individual papers â creates a sociological false positive. If 1,000 papers rule out various WIMP masses, that is not 1,000 exclusions; that is one framework failing 1,000 times. The coherence score must measure framework exhaustion, not paper count.

**Operational definition of a "theoretical framework":** A framework is a distinct set of foundational assumptions governing how an entity operates. Test: if you can move from Hypothesis A to Hypothesis B simply by turning a dial (changing a parameter, mass, cross-section, or energy level), they belong to the same framework. If you have to rewrite the base equations, introduce a new fundamental force, or change the Lagrangian, you have entered a new framework.

**The Categorical Exhaustion Ratio (CER):**

1. Catalog every distinct theoretical framework that has produced confirmed negatives for the problem.
2. Assign each framework to its presumed ontological category (Actor, Force, Determinant, Component).
3. CER = (Number of excluded frameworks in dominant category) / (Total number of excluded frameworks).

**Worked example â Dark matter excluria:**

| Framework | Result | Category |
|---|---|---|
| WIMPs (Supersymmetry) | Excluded across major parameter space | Actor |
| MACHOs (Baryonic astrophysics) | Excluded as primary component by microlensing | Actor |
| Sterile neutrinos | Excluded by X-ray background limits | Actor |
| Axions (Peccei-Quinn symmetry) | Heavily constrained, largely excluded in standard ranges | Actor |
| MOND (Modified Newtonian Dynamics) | Fails cluster dynamics, Bullet Cluster | Force/Mechanism |
| TeVeS (Tensor-Vector-Scalar gravity) | Ruled out by gravitational wave speed (GW170817) | Force/Mechanism |

CER: 4 Actor frameworks out of 6 total = 0.67. The formavacua is split (67% Actor, 33% Force). Under strict criteria this is ambiguous â the Actor concentration is below the 75% threshold. This tells the analyst that physics has not only been looking for particles; it has also been looking for forces. The versio might route toward a blend of Capacium and Processium, which aligns with modern theories mapping dark matter as a fluid or spacetime emergent property.

**CER thresholds:**
- CER > 0.75: Coherent formavacua. Versio fires.
- CER 0.50â0.75: Ambiguous. Proceed with caution, flag the split.
- CER < 0.50: Incoherent. Method does not apply.

### 9.3 Independence Requirement

The research programmes producing the negatives must be theoretically independent. Three experiments based on the same theoretical framework testing different parameter values are ONE programme, not three. Three experiments based on different theoretical frameworks (e.g., particle dark matter, modified gravity, dark fluid models) are three programmes. The CER enforces this by counting frameworks, not papers.

### 9.4 Density Requirement

The excluria must contain confirmed negatives from at least 3 independent frameworks (not individual experiments â frameworks). Below this threshold, the pattern may be noise.

### 9.5 The Property-Violation Test (External Discriminator)

After the versio fires, apply this check before acting on the output:

**Compositional exclusions** (the excluria eliminates known instances, types, or compositions within a category) â flag elevated false-positive risk. The answer may be a novel instance in the same layer. Example: prion case â all known pathogen *types* excluded, but the concept of "pathogen" was never challenged.

**Functional exclusions** (the excluria eliminates the defining functional properties of the category itself) â the versio is more likely to represent genuine layer mismatch. Example: cell size â the concept of "single localized controller" was eliminated, not just specific controllers.

This test is not a gate. It is a risk flag. Compositional exclusions do not automatically invalidate the versio â they indicate that the analyst should weight the output with lower confidence and consider the incomplete-taxonomy alternative more seriously.

These criteria are provisional. The numbers (75%, 50%, 3 frameworks) are starting points based on the cases examined in this paper, not empirically optimized thresholds.

## 10. Ontological Layer Mismatch as the Core Claim

The deepest version of Void Cartography's claim is this:

Some problems are hard not because the answer is hidden but because the question is asked at the wrong ontological layer. The instruments, the languages, the funding structures, the cognitive defaults â all of them point at one layer (typically: objects, forces, mechanisms, discrete causes). When the answer lives at a different layer (enabling conditions, systemic processes, emergent properties, relational structures), the entire research apparatus produces failure symmetry without anyone recognizing the pattern.

### 10.1 Formal Definition of Ontological Layer

An *ontological layer* is a set of entities that share the same causal role type with respect to a given problem domain. The causal role type is determined by three questions about how the entity relates to the phenomenon of interest:

1. **Locus of determination:** Does the entity set the state of the phenomenon (determinant), carry it as a passenger (enabler), arise from it as a consequence (emergent), or connect its components (relational)?
2. **Temporal priority:** Does the entity exist before the phenomenon (enabling condition), during it (participant/process), or after it (consequence)?
3. **Causal mode:** Does the entity act directly (agent/force), act indirectly by providing conditions (capacity), or not act at all but merely arise (emergent property)?

**Operational layer taxonomy:**

| Layer | Causal Role | Temporal Priority | Causal Mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| Actor | Sets state by interacting | During | Direct agent |
| Force/Mechanism | Sets state by pushing | During | Direct cause |
| Determinant/Controller | Sets state by regulating | Prior or during | Indirect via rule |
| Capacium | Enables possibility | Prior to phenomenon | Condition |
| Processium | Carries as flow | During | Systemic flow |
| Emergentium | Arises from system | After (consequent) | None (consequence) |
| Relatio | Connects components | Simultaneous | Correlation without direct cause |

**Decision tree for coding exclusions:** Given a set of excluded entities, each analyst independently codes each exclusion:

1. Is the excluded entity a thing that acts directly on the phenomenon? â **Actor.** If no:
2. Is it a thing that pushes or determines via a known force law? â **Force/Mechanism.** If no:
3. Is it a thing that sets a value or state via a rule or control loop? â **Determinant.** If no:
4. Does it exist before the phenomenon and make it possible without participating? â **Capacium.** If no:
5. Does it carry the phenomenon as a flow or movement? â **Processium.** If no:
6. Does it arise from the phenomenon as a statistical or collective consequence? â **Emergentium.** If no:
7. Does it connect two or more components without being a component itself? â **Relatio.** If none: **Uncodable.**

**Inter-rater agreement requirement:** On a training set of 50 exclusions from diverse fields, two independent analysts should achieve Cohen's kappa >= 0.80. The definition is not considered validated until this is demonstrated.

**Known boundary challenge:** The distinction between Actor and Determinant is fuzzy. A transcription factor is both an Actor (it binds DNA) and a Determinant (it regulates expression). The decision tree asks: "Is the primary excluded role that of a direct participant in the phenomenon, or a regulator of participants?" This is subtle and will produce some disagreement. The definition must be problem-relative: the same entity can belong to different layers in different problem domains. That is acceptable as long as analysts agree on the problem frame first.

Void Cartography does one thing: it reads the failure symmetry and says "try a different layer." The versio taxonomy says which layer to try. Everything else â the specific hypotheses about dark matter or cell size or quantum entanglement â is illustration, not proof.

This is an epistemological claim that entails ontological hypotheses. It says something about how to search, and that search-redirection generates specific, falsifiable predictions about what exists. The epistemological method produces ontological outputs. The outputs are hypotheses, not conclusions.

## 11. Connection to Existing Frameworks

**Renormalization (physics)** demonstrates that behavior emerges from scale-dependent structure rather than intrinsic object-properties. Universality shows different microscopic systems producing identical macroscopic behavior near phase transitions. This is the physics of emergence from layers: the layer you measure at determines what you see. Void Cartography is an epistemological method for detecting when the measurement layer is wrong.

**Inverse problems** reconstruct hidden structure from constraint patterns. Void Cartography shares this logic but adds a move inverse problems do not make: detecting when the hidden structure is at a different ontological layer than the constraints. Standard inverse methods assume the latent structure has the same ontological type as the observations. The seismic anisotropy case (Section 4) demonstrates what happens when that assumption fails â and what happens when the ontological type is corrected.

**Laudan (1977)** provided the concept of research traditions carrying shared deep commitments across multiple theories. Failure symmetry is the diagnostic pattern that emerges when those shared commitments produce coordinated failure at the type level.

**Stanford (2006)** provided the explanation for why scientists fail to conceive viable alternatives outside their shared assumptions. The prion case is a direct instance: the entire pathogen taxonomy was exhausted, and no one conceived of a protein-based genetic element because the assumption "genetic material requires nucleic acid" was invisible.

**Wimsatt (1980s)** provided robustness reasoning â independent convergence raises confidence. Failure symmetry is the negative mirror: independent failure in the same ontological category raises suspicion about the category itself.

**Kuhn (1962)** provided historical evidence that category changes solve stuck problems. Void Cartography provides a prospective method for detecting when a category change is needed, using the excluria that Kuhn's anomalies leave behind.

**Lakatos (1978)** provided the concept of research programme trajectory. Void Cartography reads trajectory patterns *across* programmes rather than within them. Failure symmetry is the cross-programme version of Lakatos's degenerating programme.

**The Whirlpool Lineage (Paper 18)** established that pattern-persistence is more fundamental than substrate. Void Cartography extends this: the spaces where patterns persist follow the same rules regardless of scale. The Realima Taxonomy's tier structure (Tier 5 enables Tier 4 enables Tier 3) is a hierarchy of nested capacia.

**The Handle (Paper 20)** addresses the naming problem that Void Cartography creates. The method finds things at layers that existing vocabulary cannot cleanly describe. ## 12. Honest Limitations

**The method is abductive.** It generates plausible hypotheses, not proofs. Every result in this paper is [Speculative].

**The method is late-stage.** It requires decades of accumulated confirmed negatives across multiple independent frameworks. It cannot help with young problems.

**The prion problem is a genuine false positive.** The method cannot distinguish "wrong layer" from "incomplete taxonomy within the correct layer." This is a structural limitation, not a fixable bug. The Property-Violation Test (Section 7.4) provides a partial discriminator but does not eliminate the risk. When the excluria eliminates known compositions rather than functional definitions, false-positive risk is elevated. But even this flag is retrospective â we identified it by studying the prion failure, not by predicting it.

**The validation set is small.** Five solved/resolved problems (H. pylori, Higgs, continental drift, prions, quantum entanglement). The 4/5 pass rate is encouraging but statistically insufficient to establish reliability. A 30-problem validation set is the minimum needed for meaningful confidence intervals (see Section 13).

**The versio taxonomy is incomplete.** Four output categories (capacium, processium, emergentium, relatio) were derived from the problems examined and anchored in existing philosophical traditions. Other categories may exist. The taxonomy should be treated as an operational starting framework, not a closed set.

**Operator dependence remains.** Classifying excluria entries into ontological categories requires judgment. The CER and decision tree (Sections 9â10) help but do not eliminate subjectivity. Different operators may read the same excluria differently. Inter-rater reliability has not been measured.

**The epistemology/ontology boundary is intentionally porous.** The method makes epistemological claims (how to search) that entail ontological hypotheses (what exists at the redirected layer). This is a feature, not a bug â but it means the method's outputs carry the uncertainty of both levels simultaneously.

**Selection bias is not resolved.** Independent blind testing is required.

## 13. Future Work

**Expand the validation set to 30 solved problems.** Balanced by type: 15 with object-shaped answers (to measure false positive rate) and 15 with non-object-shaped answers (to measure false negative rate). Each problem must have a clear historical record of when it was solved and what the excluria looked like before solution. Only problems solved at least 10 years ago. Target thresholds: false positive rate <= 15% (at most 2-3 of 15 object-problems misclassified as non-object); false negative rate <= 20% (at most 3 of 15 non-object problems missed). The current 1/5 false positive rate (20%) exceeds the target; the expanded set will determine whether this is representative or inflated by small sample size.

**Blind testing.** Provide the protocol and decision tree to analysts uninvolved in its development. Give them a mixed set of solved and unsolved problems. Measure agreement between operators (target: Cohen's kappa >= 0.80) and accuracy against known answers.

**Calibrate the CER thresholds.** The 75%/50% coherence thresholds and the 3-framework minimum are starting points. Apply the CER to 30+ solved problems and optimize thresholds against the false-positive/false-negative trade-off. Determine whether the CER performs better than raw paper-counting at predicting method accuracy.

**Validate the Property-Violation Test.** Apply the compositional/functional exclusion distinction to all 30 validation problems. Determine whether it reliably flags false positives without suppressing true positives. Measure its discriminative power as an independent metric.

**Expand Relatio validation.** Quantum entanglement is one case. Candidates for additional testing: ecosystem resilience (connectivity between species), neural binding problem (integration across distributed neural populations), social network effects (where the network structure itself is the explanatory target). Apply the method and verify whether the formavacua routes to Relatio.

**Cross-domain application.** Consciousness (massive excluria, long-stuck). Quantum measurement problem. Abiogenesis. Economic value. Test whether the method works outside natural science.

**Formalize the ontological layer decision tree.** The seven-layer taxonomy with three diagnostic questions (Section 10) needs empirical calibration. Produce a training set of 50+ exclusions from diverse fields, have independent analysts code them, measure inter-rater reliability, and refine the decision criteria at boundary cases.
